## Design Context

### Users
Friends or online opponents who want to open a room quickly, share an invite link, and play Gomoku in real time on desktop or mobile. They need clear turn state, low-friction room joining, and confident feedback for moves, undo requests, wins, and reconnections.

### Brand Personality
Refined, tactile, focused. The experience should feel like a quiet evening game room: polished wood, precise stones, soft light, and calm competitive tension.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark, warm, board-game atmosphere with a detailed wood board and dimensional black/white stones. The interface should be app-first, not a landing page. Controls stay compact and functional; the board is the star. Avoid generic neon dashboards, purple gradients, and oversized marketing sections.

### Design Principles
- Make the playable board the first visual signal on every viewport.
- Keep the room workflow simple: create, join by code, or share invite link.
- Preserve parity across desktop and mobile; no core action disappears on phones.
- Use tactile visual detail for board, pieces, shadows, and last-move state.
- Keep motion purposeful and respect reduced-motion preferences.
